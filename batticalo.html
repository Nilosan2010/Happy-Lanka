Batticalo Fort

The Batticaloa Fort, was built by the Portuguese in 1628 and was captured by the Dutch on 18 May 1638. 
From 1795, the fort was used by the British.

The fort has a structure of four bastions and is protected by the Batticaloa Lagoon on two sides and a canal on the other two sides. 
The fort is still in reasonable condition and currently houses several local administrative departments of the Sri Lanka government in new buildings, 
which are located within the old structure.


Timeline of Batticaloa fort in colonial time.

1622 – Construction began by Portuguese
1628 – Construction completed
1638 – Dutch captured
1639 – Fort destroyed by Dutch
1665 – Reconstruction started
1682 – Renovation
1707 – Front bastion and complex completed
1766 – Ceded to Kandyan kingdom
1796 – Captured by British
